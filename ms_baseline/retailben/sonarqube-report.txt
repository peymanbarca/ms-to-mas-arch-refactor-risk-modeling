
Cognitive complexity is usually lower than cyclomatic complexity in well-written microservice codebases.

Because microservices are designed to be small, single-purpose, and loosely coupled, their internal codebase naturally leans toward 
    a linear architecture that maps cleanly to how human brains process information. Microservices inherently avoid the deep,
    tangled logic structures that cause cognitive complexity to explode. 

If you find that a specific microservice has a higher cognitive complexity than cyclomatic complexity,
 it is a strong code smell.
 It usually means that business logic is heavily nested, or that the microservice has grown too large and is turning back into a "mini-monolith."
